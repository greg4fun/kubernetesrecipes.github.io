## Kubernetes Recipes Book Giveaway at KubeCon EU 2026

KubeCon EU 2026 in London was a milestone — not just for presenting **"Lessons Learned Orchestrating Multi-Tenant GPUs on OpenShift AI"**, but for the incredible response to the **Kubernetes Recipes** book giveaway.

### The Talk

The session covered real-world lessons from deploying NVIDIA GPU infrastructure at scale on OpenShift with KAI scheduler, GH200 Grace Hopper, and multi-tenant GPU sharing. The room was packed — standing room only — and the questions didn't stop for 20 minutes after the session ended.

Key topics that sparked the most discussion:

- **KAI Scheduler** topology-aware GPU placement
- **MIG reconfiguration** on live clusters without node drain
- **SR-IOV RDMA** for NCCL inter-node GPU communication
- **GPU Feature Discovery** for heterogeneous GPU fleets

### The Giveaway

After the talk, we gave away signed copies of **Kubernetes Recipes** to attendees:

- **50+ copies** distributed across the conference
- Books handed out at the talk, at the booth, and in the hallway track
- Every copy was signed with a personal note
- Some copies went to attendees who traveled from Asia and South America — the global reach of the community never ceases to amaze me

### Hallway Track Highlights

The best conversations at KubeCon always happen in the hallway. Here's what people were talking about:

- **Distributed inference** — running 100B+ parameter models across multiple GPU nodes
- **NCCL optimization** — IB vs RoCE vs TCP, GPU-Direct RDMA, PXN topology
- **Cost management** — GPU hours are expensive; teams need better scheduling and bin-packing
- **Platform engineering** — internal developer platforms with GPU self-service
- **CNCF AI landscape** — KAITO, KServe, vLLM, and the explosion of AI-native K8s tools

### Community Impact

The Kubernetes Recipes project has grown significantly since KubeCon:

- **1200+ free recipes** on [kubernetes.recipes](https://kubernetes.recipes)
- **400+ clicks per month** from Google Search (and growing)
- Coverage spanning networking, storage, security, AI/GPU, observability, and CNCF ecosystem
- The book complements the site with deeper, structured learning paths
